History of Hogwarts

According to legend, Hogwarts was founded by four of the greatest wizards and witches of their time: Godric Gryffindor, Salazar Slytherin, Helga Hufflepuff, and Rowena Ravenclaw. They each brought their own unique skills and talents to the creation of the school. Together, these four founders created Hogwarts as a place where young witches and wizards could learn magic and develop their skills. They each contributed magical objects to create the Sorting Hat, which sorted incoming students into one of the four houses based on their personality traits and values. Over the centuries, Hogwarts grew and changed. The castle itself was expanded and updated many times, with new rooms and wings added to accommodate the growing number of students. The curriculum also evolved, with new courses and teachers added as magical knowledge advanced. Despite these changes, Hogwarts remained a beloved and respected institution of magical learning. It continued to produce talented and skilled witches and wizards who went on to make important contributions to the wizarding world.

Campus Activities

Hogwarts offers a wide range of extracurricular activities for its students to explore and develop their interests outside of class, including Quidditch, Dueling Club, Gobstones Club, Orchestra, Frog Choir, and many more!
